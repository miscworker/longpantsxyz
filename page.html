An important order came in the mail today: an attachable antenna for my computer.

The question as to whether or not Isabelle was being tracked or emitting a signal had to be answered if she was going to continue living in my apartment.

The answer is no. There’s no signal coming from her whatsoever.

It was anticlimactic. Even disappointing, actually, as it muddles the mystery to her even further. But at least I don’t have to worry about that anymore.


---


Isabelle wanted to try cooking today. I’m thankful that she waited until I was around so that I didn’t come home to a burnt-up kitchen. After using my computer to research recipes, she settled on fish stew with salmon. I’m no chef myself, so the most I could assist her with was getting the ingredients and making sure she knew how to use the stove. There was also a shortage of cooking utensils and other items I had to stock the kitchen with… It’s a miracle I’m not a fatter person from solely eating out, but I suppose I’ve lost my appetite over the years anyway.

Since I could only demonstrate so much for her, Isabelle realized the miracle that is the internet and its ability to provide her with whatever information she needed. She watched video tutorials for every step in preparation and repeatedly asked if she was doing things right. She absorbed everything well enough; her biggest strength I’ve noticed so far is how receptive she is to everything. I guess that could balance out into being a flaw if she finds an interest in something not particularly rewarding.

When the stew was finished, she took a spoon and tasted it. The joy which appeared in her eyes was like a sun appearing through a brief opening in overcast; when all the effort pulls through and success prevails, and the journey fulfilled makes the destination so much more pleasurable.

I feel even a little jealous that she can experience something so wonderful.

The food tasted wonderful. Maybe not so much that it was actually done well, but more so because it was cooked at home and I witnessed it, both of which I haven’t experienced for unreasonably too long.

I’ve noticed that when Isabelle eats, she looks intently at her food. Not in any gluttonous way, but rather curiously. She seemed much more intelligent about it with her own cooking, as if studying its intricacies would lead her to improve her own meals in the future. When she’d bite it, she ate slowly and sometimes closed her eyes and seemed lost in thought. She caught me staring one of the times after opening her eyes and laughed it off.

I told her afterwards that I’ll buy her whatever she needed for cooking.
