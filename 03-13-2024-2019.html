There was something peculiar about Isabelle’s appearance that I’ve been trying to figure out since I met her.

Today I’ve finally found out: it’s the blue fur.

After reading over the Aiom Corp’s scientific manual to Aiom biology and genetics - a public overview on all of the possibilities and limitations to Aiom appearances - I verified that there shouldn’t be Aioms of most blue hues for fur, assuming the genetic alteration is drawn from the limitations of animals found in nature. Blue already is a rare color in nature, and the few uses of it are barely found in mammals, of which are more of a grayish hue.

This of course is in the context of mammal fur. The colors of other animals such as blue jays aren't worth considering, as their color is an illusion made possible by light scattering. Isabelle’s fur retains its color even no matter what I do to it (She lent me a small shedding from the back of her head to test with). I even tried washing it out of a potential dye, but it remained the same.

There are some, though very few, animals in nature that do produce true blue pigmentation in their appearance. However, this doesn’t explain why Isabelle is the only truly blue (Even if closer to a sky-blue color than to violet) Aiom that I’ve ever encountered. I scrubbed through the internet for an Aiom that could have a similar color. Nothing.

Today, I asked one of the scientists at Aiom Corp about the possibility, withholding the situation with Isabelle. He said there was no practical way to apply true blue pigmentation to anthro Aioms, given the original outline used for the current genetic engineering standard created years ago. Interestingly, he said that it’s common for customers to request for an Aiom of a blue hue, only to be disappointed by the impossibility of it.

When I asked if there’s a specific blue at all, he said there’s been a lot more requests for ‘lighter ones, maybe pastel blue’. He even was able to pull up an example of a request in the form of a consumer quote from only a few months ago, which provided an exact hex code of a color (Of which is only a reference for developing the Aiom; Aioms are never going to reasonably be the same exact digital value of a color as requested).

Now that I look at it, pastel blue is a more accurate color to describe Isabelle’s fur as. I’d pin it down as closest to #C1E5FF in typical lighting conditions. When she’s outside in the sun, she glows. I actually find myself a little mesmerized by her, the feeling multiplied by her mysterious nature.

Isabelle likes to sit at Athen’s Square, her currently favorite park. When it is a clear day, she glimmers. Her bell joins the ambience of birds chirping and people talking. The moment is so mundane, but she looks at the checkered floor and statues like it might be her last chance every time.

Oh, how I envy her.
