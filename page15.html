Isabelle asked if she could come along to work today.

Interestingly enough, employees are allowed to take their Aiom companions onto the work campus. Having your partner accompany you at work apparently makes you a lot happier. It would have been an interesting experience for her, but I don’t like the idea of taking an illegally made Aiom to the company that produces them. Her tail was just too obvious of an issue, and I still haven’t tended to her horns.

She then asked if I could stay around instead of going into work. I didn’t want her to become bored again, so I took her to the local library and showed her how she can find whatever she’d like to read, and that she can maybe discover some new interests to pursue. I left her with my phone number in case she had to call me from the library, some cash to eat at a restaurant across the street if she became hungry - stressing to her to not order more than the thirty dollars I gave her - and directions to wait for me after work to take her home. I wasn’t entirely confident in letting her find her way home just yet.

Nothing came about at work until three in the afternoon, when I received a call from an unknown number. I assumed it was Isabelle from the library, but it was instead Isabelle on a cell phone all the way up in the Bronx. Apparently she went on a bit of an adventure, something I wasn’t filled in on until I picked her up and we took the train home.

According to her, she browsed for books and read for a few hours, while also asking people for help in looking for ‘interesting things so she can learn to help people.’ Apparently that meant asking everyone including regular patrons at the library going about their business. Eventually one of the librarians had to ask her to stop bothering others, so Isabelle asked her for assistance.

However it was that Isabelle phrased it, the librarian suggested that she explored volunteer work. Today, a woman by the name of Elizabeth was collecting local compost from the library’s dropoff to take to a playground near Mosholu Parkway in the Bronx. Isabelle offered a hand and accompanied Elizabeth up there and spent the remainder of her time helping her dig up and plant seeds and soil. She then called me on Elizabeth’s phone.

The phone call gave me no information except the address to pick her up from and that she was fine and busy doing volunteer work (She said that phrase very proudly - ‘volunteer work’). I assumed it would’ve been a public location when I arrived, but it was instead Elizabeth’s townhome where she invited Isabelle in for coffee.

I went from stressed out as hell to relieved but irritated as hell. I suppose she isn’t going to listen flawlessly to anything I tell her either, but this is the sort of behavior I’m trying to witness. It’s just stressful having a bit of an uncontrollable research project walking around. I still don’t know if she might be sought after by anyone for whatever reason.

But it went as peacefully as a change in plans could go. Elizabeth is an older woman who tends to the nearby parks and food pantry, which Isabelle said she wants to go and volunteer at next. She is nice and I have nothing to complain about her at all, but my first question to her was - in a private conversation - if it was normal in any world for her to let who she assumes is someone else’s Aiom come with her to do volunteer work.

To that, she said, something of the sort, “She didn’t say she was anyone’s Aiom. Or even an Aiom at all, for that matter. So if she wanted to help, I’m not going to turn it down. It’s not as if there’s a line for these things.”

It was a bit of a shocker to meet someone who was able to accept the humanity of an Aiom. Or perhaps she, no offense, was simply amused by Isabelle’s behavior. In this instance, I’ll be optimistic and assume the former. She asked if Isabelle can come around in the future, to which I responded that it’s entirely up to her.

After she told me her little adventure on the way home (Here it is saved of the details of moving the compost and planting various seeds in a community garden), I asked if she knowingly left the area in Queens despite me asking her not to. She said she completely forgot. I explained to her how worried I was when she said she was elsewhere, stopping myself when I realized how possessive I sounded, then I asked if she could stay put until Saturday, when I’ll show her how to navigate the city without me. She agreed.

“Why are you so worried for me?” she asked, “I don’t see what there is to be afraid of here.”

After looking for a way to phrase it, I came up with, “Because you’re the most interesting thing to enter my life in a while, and I can’t bear to see you go away before I fully understand you.”

“Ohh. Okay. I’m sorry.”

She then told me, upon being asked, that she gave her thirty dollars to a man asking for money outside. Elizabeth said she has the heart of an angel.

I say she is an impressionable dork.
