Recent news that I have been invested in surrounds the inclusion of AI in arts and entertainment.

I play no part in the arts or entertainment industry, but I am certainly an artist in the world of programming. Such work was deemed to be threatened by AI ever since it gained traction through public accessible programs, but any sensitive work involving the safety of individuals is just too risky to put into the hands of an unintelligent mind.

Though, such a statement begs the question of what the definition of an intelligent mind is, let alone a mind at all.

Point is: my job, in all of the past ten years of increasing AI use, has never been threatened by it. If anything were to change, it would simply degrade into reviewing work of automatically generated code, which, in my opinion, would require so much correction that my job title would be the only actual change.

In the entertainment industry, though, goodness. What hell it must be for artists right now.

Despite the layoffs and unsuccessful strikes, I think art is far from dead. The entertainment industry is destined to strive for maximum efficiency in product making - it is an industry, in the end. The inclusion of creative liberty and the arts does not make it any less of an assembly line than a factory processing and boxing milk into cartons. Art itself though, however, adapts and evolves, as always.

In fact, here is a controversial opinion of mine: it is good that this has happened to the entertainment industry. Even before the use of AI, workers were subjected to unfair conditions and practices at all levels, all just to produce a ridiculous amount of garbage media that goes unnoticed or immediately forgotten. Now that AI is used to speed up the process, what has changed? That the products they produce are now even more noticeably low effort?

I admit that my opinion is biased by the fact that I find the world overwhelming. However, I think we can all agree that there is little artistic pride in any piece of media that is manufactured for profit. If people wish to find meaningful creations made by humans instead of machines, all they need to do is look for, well, actual art films. No, they wonâ€™t be full of CGI nonsense that people apparently need in every one of their movies nowadays, but they will definitely have more heart than the average summer blockbuster. More than that though, look locally and you will find art in all sorts of places. If there is none to be found, be an artist yourself and make something. It is in our nature, after all, to leave our mark on the world.

Not to consume the mark, turned into a consumable product, of a corporation. We need to be made more aware of the factory-produced products that the average product of the entertainment industry is.

Programming and most technical things are usually only considered artforms by people within its circle. My job is far from ever being considered art by the public view.

But at least I can take pride knowing that my work, which is well respected by my peers, is unique, applicable, and is made entirely by me. In a patch, my solution to a problem is sent to Aioms all over the country. It is a mark that goes unnoticed, but it is still there. In a way, my presence exceeds that of the average person. My influence is not grand, perhaps, but underestimated.
