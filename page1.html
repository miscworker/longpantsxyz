I realize, from writing on here, that, whenever I finish and upload the post, I have little else to do, especially on a Sunday. Not that venting into a void is necessarily the sort of therapy I was looking for, but it does interest me to see my thoughts otherwise unsaid be written in a format for myself and others to return to.

I dread Sundays for contradictory reasons: returning to work feels like a drag, but I have nothing to make use of my time anymore, so I end up slogging around the house or outside. The idea of doing nothing never made much sense to me, but I envy those who find peace in it. On the other hand, going into work means facing the forefront of the reality I despise so much.

I realize now that it’s probably confusing that I work at Aiom Corp while despising Aioms. I swear that there is a good reason for it.

There is a lot to clarify in my relationship with Aioms. I do not hate Aioms themselves. It is not their fault that they are created from a laboratory only to be fitted with a microcomputer and have their lives stolen. What I hate, rather, is what they thus represent due to their existence: the oblivious consumer world which will produce and justify just about anything as long as it sells. This is obnoxious enough with products such as vapes and junk foods, which are designed to be addictive, but the fact that Aiom Corp openly advertises a companion which can replace all relationships you will ever need, and even friendships for those socially deprived on an ultra level, is utterly dystopian. We have entered the state of computer technology in which it replaces aspects of human interaction on a level obvious to the average (ignorant) person.

At one point though, I was unaware of all this. I thought Aioms existed in the same way all innovations seem to exist in the perspective of an ignorant person: because ‘why not?’ Since I was young, I have been entranced by the inner workings of artificial intelligence in human interaction (AI in work such as statistics, data collection, content generation, etc. don’t intrigue me so much). I went to college up to my master’s in AI computer programming and engineering with the dream of working at Aiom Corp, and it all actually worked out as well as it could. They were seeking freshly graduated recruits at the time and selected me to enlist in their academy.

Yes, Aiom Corp has its own academy for considering what you should even do in their company. Like the rest of school, I finished at the top of their three month course, and my superiors had me fly out here to New York to work in their office in person. It all couldn’t have been more convenient - I was able to escape the suburban, culture-eating hellhole that was my home, move to an amazing city, plant myself immediately in the company I wanted to work in, and all while affording to do so. Hell, Aiom Corp had even bought the Empire State Building years prior, and that’s my favorite building in this entire city (They take heavy precautions in keeping all their work secret and secure. Remote work is not an option if you handle Aiom source code in any shape or form. If there is a blizzard, my office just closes entirely.)

There was virtually not a single issue in the world with my life changing as it did ten years ago. I suppose my father being disappointed was one of them, but I stopped caring about his opinion in my late teens. My mother supported me as long as I was happy.

I don’t know when it was that I realized that Aioms were not good for America. I was aware of their creation process and whatnot for a while, long before ever working at Aiom Corp. It is all public information, although Aiom Corp never refers to the process in ways that might allude to technological slavery or anything reminiscent of a dystopian novel (Even the acronym for ‘Aiom’, which is ‘Artificially Intelligent Organic Machine,’ is omitted from everything in public media except for disclaimers and technical documentation).

I do recall a disturbing news story from about seven years ago that stuck with me ever since I heard about it. A man, forty years old at the time, purchased an Aiom three years before the story and had her taken to an off-market modifier to discreetly tamper with the pain network circuitry in her microcomputer.

Aiom lesson for context: The pain network circuitry intercepts signals from the nervous system in an Aiom which would otherwise go to the appropriate parts of the brain (It is several locations, where exactly I forget) and signal pain. The interception does not make them unable to detect pain, but rather it nullifies their intense reactions unless it is a severe enough injury. It also intercepts them in order to catalog possible abuse they receive from their owner, which is then shared with the Online Aiom Network, which tracks their wellbeing and if any measures have to be taken. So if an Aiom were to stub its toe (Which would be an indication of a movement or tracking malfunction considering they are electronically assisted), it wouldn’t overreact the same way a regular person would but it would still be aware of it. If it had its arm severed, it would make a fuss but just to bring awareness to its injury. If it were being deliberately attacked, it would defend itself and the OA Network would be notified.

It can be imagined what sort of intent someone who wishes to discreetly disable an Aiom’s connection with the OA network in regards to its pain would have. I believe he was reported by a resident in the same building as seeing him throwing out something bloody in the early morning hours. When authorities entered his apartment, they found the Aiom woman - a feline furry model, apparently in the same pattern of a childhood cat he had, chained to a wall with not only her microcomputer tampered with as I described, but as well as her ability to speak entirely. Her pawed feet were severed and located in the garbage he threw away hours before; her body was littered with deep cuts and lacerations, many damaged from applied liquids. She was equipped with an IV attached to a gallon of company-grade Aiom nutrition fluid to feed her as her body struggled to regenerate itself. Considering the genetically enhanced speed at which Aioms are able to recover at least cuts and bruises, the fact that she was covered with so many still meant that they could have been made only within the hour upon discovering her.

The Aiom was registered as a romantic companion, unnamed.

After that story came out, there was quite a bit of controversy around Aiom Corp, though obviously not the first to come around. My office floor, who were all fellow Aiom personality programmers, were briefed on figuring out alternative methods to allow Aioms to recognize when they are in danger or if they are witnessing criminal behavior. Honestly, I was surprised that this had not been addressed sooner. If one of the main concerns of Aioms is the safety of humans, I can only imagine what ones that have been illegally modified are behaving like out of public view.

One can probably imagine the difficulty of programming a guarantee of separation in understanding danger and make-believe (A very, very large number of Aiom owners purchase the product for BDSM purposes). A recent method in training Aiom AI is to not actually train their programming itself, but to train the already intelligent brains they have through actual classes before they are rolled out to consumers. It is a bizarre scene to witness: Aioms of different genders, sizes, cross-species sitting at desks and watching videos and listening to an instructor on recognizing danger versus bedroom fun. The efforts do help, but it is much more cost-efficient for a genius or two to figure out the right algorithms and few lines of code to teach the necessary knowledge to all Aioms through simple patch and an hour or two of coma-induced AI training. If you are not an Aiom owner and you’ve ever wondered why one day there suddenly aren’t as many scantily-dressed women or unusual looking people outside, it’s likely because an Aiom software patch was rolled out and they are all at home sleeping while their computer plays subliminal messages into their brain to finalize their update.

That story always stuck with me like a thorn, though. I knew people could absolutely use Aioms for bad, just like any other piece of technology. But at the same time, they are alive, and demented people realize that themselves and all the things they could do with that information. I consider myself one of those demented people, but for reasons which aren’t widely agreed with.

I don’t believe that story triggered any sort of epiphany for me about Aioms. The awareness was gradual until it was one day realized at no particular moment - probably five years ago I can say I was fully aware. However, leaving my job would have and still be a huge issue for me. If I give up my job and my salary, I would definitely have to move out of my current home in Astoria, the third floor in a beautiful brownstone I refuse to abandon. I would have to leave the city, and, as much as I hate everything happening and the constant day-to-day exposure of Aioms, I can’t do that. I’m not prepared yet, anyway.

Not only that, but it is my responsibility to understand the dystopia encroaching upon the world. If I never joined Aiom Corp, I never would have received the wealth of knowledge on artificial intelligence that I have now. I at least am able to ensure that Aioms are safe for people if they must be used, not that I’d ever consider myself or anyone else a hero for that.

But if I am to ever be a hero and save America, I will need first-hand experience with the enemy.

… There is also the fact that, again, I do enjoy working with artificial intelligence.

I just did not plan on employing my abilities in designing the behavior of an average sex-addicted anthropomorphic character turned to life.
