I managed to actually sleep for a few hours today, but not before being awoken by Isabelle’s movement in the living space.

Today was honestly normal, if I was a normal person anyway. I don’t mean that in any pretentious way - if anything, I wish I was more average. Rather, it was a weird day for myself in the end by being more normal.

Isabelle came with a whole list of errands I had to take care of: she needed new clothes, there was no food in the house, she wanted to clean up the house but couldn’t because I didn’t have any cleaning supplies. She didn't literally tell me to provide her of all these things, but her complaints were obvious. I told her I would get everything for her, though there wasn’t any practical way for me to purchase clothes for her alone without having her accompany me outside. I was nervous about having her be seen outside, considering her horns were yet to be dulled and her tail was undoubtedly oversized, but once she was in public, nobody seemed to care.

Of course, considering her tail, I had to take her to an Aiom clothing outlet, the logic of which she couldn’t comprehend. She insisted that there would be clothes for her at any woman’s clothing store. The easiest way I found to answer her questions was to just say “It doesn’t work like that around here.”

“This city is weird,” she said at one point.

At the Aiom store itself - the one in Brooklyn at the mall area around Atlantic Avenue - there was thankfully an assistant (Also an Aiom… The economy is so bizarre now) willing to help her find something. The assistant kept returning to me asking what I wanted Isabelle to wear after she wasn’t able to give a straight answer, so I just told them to suggest things for her and see if she likes it.

I left her for almost an hour to buy groceries and other things. Upon returning, I found her in a new robe, something stylized after a sci-fi fantasy setting. The rest of her chosen clothes were robes as well, equally unusual. I asked if she’d rather choose something more casual. She didn’t see the problem. She explained also that she wasn’t comfortable in anything tight-fitting like jeans or shirts, which I couldn’t contest, even if I was the one paying for it. But there are so many more conventional things she could have chosen from… If only people didn’t decorate their Aioms with so many fantasy elements.

She didn’t even want shoes, since apparently she found any she put on to be too tight. I didn’t want her walking around the city barefoot though, so I helped her find sandals instead, which she was able to tolerate.

I think she looks like a complete dork, but what Aioms are. A bunch of dorks in cosplay.

But she was happy with it. So much so that she randomly stopped someone on the street to tell them about how nice I was for getting her new clothes and giving her a place to live and whatever else. I assured the stranger that I did not make her say any of that. He gave me a weird look, as if I was the weird one in this city.

The train trip back home was cramped. I watched Isabelle look around at all the oddities curiously, mainly the Aioms. She left my side and wandered through the cart, shamelessly making eye contact with whoever she observed next and entering their personal space. I couldn’t hear the conversation completely, but she spoke with an Aiom - a human female model with a shelf of a chest - for a moment, then with her owner - a stumpy looking man, the sort that was likely to smell like smoke. She returned to my side before being swept away with the moving crowd.

“What did you say to them?” I asked.

“That woman is so gorgeous, I just had to complement her. I’ve never seen a woman like that before,” she said, something of the sort.

“They’re everywhere here. That woman, I’ve seen three copies of her in the same cart before.”

“What? How?”

“She’s an Aiom. A popular model.”

She looked off in thought.

“Do you or do you not know what Aioms are?”

“Not really.”

“You said you weren’t an Aiom, though,” I said in a whisper.

“Well, I know I’m not an Aiom, of course. I’m a person,” she spoke loudly, unaware of the weight in her words. A few folks turned their heads.

“Quiet for a moment,” I told her.

When we left the train at our stop in Astoria, a man came to my side and asked if he heard Isabelle correctly. I immediately became nervous and told him I didn’t know what he was talking about, but he pushed and asked if I was able to disable her Aiom awareness, and if I would be willing to do the same for his Aioms. Isabelle was about to interject when I yelled at the guy to back off. Thank God nobody cares when someone yells in the station. I feel like I look insane in any situation where I have to raise my voice. It doesn’t help that I’m pulling Isabelle along like a parent.

Isabelle was confused about the interaction when we arrived home, so I tried to run through her knowledge on Aioms one more time: as she said earlier, she didn’t know what an Aiom was whatsoever. I asked why she acted like she understood me on the concept when we first met.

“I assumed you knew more than me, so I listened,” she answered.

“And yet you don’t believe me when I say that you are an Aiom?”

“I’ve no reason to believe it.”

I explained the specifics of Aioms, and how I work at the sole manufacturer of them even. She still disagreed after all that, even when I compared her to the appearance of Aioms.

“What do you think about the possibility that you’ve been modified to deny your Aiom identity?”

“I don’t think anything of it except that it’s wrong.”

She asked about my intentions in relation to believing that she is an Aiom. I said that, in order to help her, I have to understand her, and these features of hers are the only lead I have at the moment. She didn’t have the memory of anything from the past to argue with me, so she just accepted the disagreement.

Then she wanted to clean. I still don’t know what her obsession with cleaning the house is, but, now that the sunlight was coming through and my attention was drawn to it, my place really was an eyesore. I don’t know why I’d ever have any pride about living in an original brownstone in Astoria when I wouldn’t even keep my place clean. Isabelle’s apparent amnesia might have blurred memories of a previous home… One that was cleaner and more welcoming. Then again, as I’ve described, she just wants to pay her debts, albeit aggressively.

I now lean away from the theory that she wanted to do chores due to the innate desire of Aiom AI to provide service. She didn’t know how to clean. The whole idea of it was vague to her, yet the concept was clearly substantial enough in her mind to bring it up. So I spent the remainder of the day helping her clean up my home while showing her all the basics. Thankfully there was almost nothing that needed showing twice, so she wasn’t dumb, something I didn’t want to see her as.

The joy she expressed from cleaning the place was quite special. I don’t know when was the last time I did something so mundane but still enjoyed it. After a few hours, she was exhausted and ready to crash again. If I didn’t know any better, I’d think that she never had to do a single chore ever. I know of Aioms who are customized to be worshiped by their owners. There is somewhat of an angelic nature I can imagine being deliberate in her design.

Or, as I often do, I’m just overthinking it. She is more likely just an Aiom that’s been rarely active and has no memory of anything.

My home’s far from perfect, but it’s much cleaner than before now. I suppose having someone to hold me accountable, or at least embarrass me for my living state, provides some benefit. I should probably just go back to having roommates again like in college, and purposefully seek out some obnoxious uptight ones who are vegan and activists, so that I can simulate the childhood of having an authority figure always judging my every decision.

Isabelle is sleeping on the couch as I type this, and I’m falling asleep myself. It’s difficult to be alone with her, let alone in the same house, so I’ll just go back to the office tomorrow. I’m still not sure what I have for her to do while I’m gone, but hopefully I figure something out before then.
