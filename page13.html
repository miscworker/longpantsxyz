I am exhausted, but I don’t want to go to sleep without writing about my experiences today.

Isabelle is definitely an unusual specimen. I say that from the perspective of a researcher. If she is really a person, well, I’ll come to regret calling her that, I’m sure.

I wasn’t able to sleep for all of Sunday morning. Isabelle awoke at around nine. In the sunlight, the mess my apartment was in was apparent enough for her to comment about it. She phrased it in such a way where it couldn’t be confirmed as a complaint - “Goodness, I hope nothing bad happened here.” I couldn’t blame her. I’ve had a hard time keeping my place clean for the past while. I guess no amount of personal struggles can excuse letting takeout containers stack up around the trash can though.

The first thing I wanted to do was plug a computer into her lone USB port - save the sexual innuendos, please - but she insisted that she wanted to eat first. She pushed back against my plan until I finally budged, and I went out to the nearest deli for a couple of breakfast platters. Before I left, I asked her to promise me that she wouldn’t leave the apartment, to which she had no problem.

So, first and foremost: Isabelle already shows more resistance than any Aiom should. While she already shows signs of submission, considering she was willing to sleep on my own couch without a second thought, the fact that she prioritized her own needs before my own wishes proves some sense of self beyond her relationship to another. Usually an Aiom would at worst warn the owner that they require substance, but never would they interfere with their owner’s plans.

When I returned home, she was cleaning up the place, throwing trash away into a garbage bag. When I asked why she felt the need to clean up, she said it was the least she could do if I am going to help her - moreso, she admitted, was that the place was too cluttered for her liking.

“Do you feel like you have the authority to do what you want here?” I asked her. I wasn’t offended, but rather curious as to how she'd react.

“I think anyone should be allowed to clean up a mess,” she said something of the sort.

She ate her breakfast quickly but politely, then finally she let me connect my laptop into her USB port (I absolutely have no idea how else to phrase this without it sounding a certain way). She insisted that the cord was inserting into nothing even as I did it.

So there is in fact something in her head to be found, according to the Aiom troubleshooter: a microcomputer of standard design for an Aiom from eleven years ago. Not a single transferable file in its two gigabytes of storage. There was no existent operating system on it, and the bios, which would’ve contained the base Aiom AI that most models have, was locked behind what could’ve been a few dozen layers of encryption, a huge red flag for criminal use. As for the purpose of this Aiom being modified, I’ll get to that at the end of this.

The computer model being eleven years old isn’t a signifier of Isabelle’s age. When I asked her age, she said she didn’t know. I’d place her apparent age as thirty-five. The incomplete, simplified computer does imply that she could be an unauthorized production model, or perhaps an Aiom used for testing and to never be released for public use.

(Aioms in these scenarios aren’t killed or discarded, of course. They are just repurposed for simple tasks, such as cleaning, within Aiom Corp or other locations. That still does not make up for how depressing of an existence they are cursed with.)

If Isabelle is, in fact, either a beta model or unauthorized model, that could also mean that she didn’t contain any actual tracking functions. I don’t have any means of verifying if she is receiving a signal from anywhere, but I’ll try and find out sooner or later.

That deadend left me with her actual appearance to evaluate, something I wasn’t too keen on doing. I’m not about to remove her robe and see what she looked like under there - whether or not she was an Aiom, nor if I was genuinely just trying to research her, it isn’t my business at all. Instead I focused on just what I saw and what I asked of her.

Her non-human features are typical for an anthro Aiom, although I’m not sure if referring to her as an anthro is fair. Her facial traits are all human: no fangs, no snout, no predatory eyes, etc. I’m not good at describing human faces, but I would call hers soft at heart but somehow roughened. I might be focusing too much on the gaze in her eyes as she followed mine, a captivating feature of hers.

The horns are standard: small curled bits at either side of her head, rooting from her skull like ornaments rather than anything similar to a real animal. They are a faded beige, barely shiny at all. A good wax and shine would probably bring out their glamor once more.

Her hands are normal human hands, lined with a thin fur coat versus the slightly thicker amount along the rest of her visible body (Visible body that I’ve examined being her arms, ankles, feet, and neck and up). On her palms where the fur receded, her skin loses pigment as normal.

Her feet are the general shape of a human’s, but she bears paws at the end of her feet instead of toes. Strangely, she is also very flat footed; I asked if it hurt her to walk here, which she did barefoot, and she said she didn’t mind it. At that point I realized I needed to have her shower as soon as possible.

The tail is probably her most interesting feature. For one, it’s oversized, both in length and thickness. I don’t know how she is able to withstand it, but it looks like there is a balance between it and her larger size(Compared to people, she is larger, but there are plenty of six foot plus Aioms around). It doesn’t wag or seem to emote, but it does slump when she’s in a relaxed sitting position, and it curls up enough to not touch the floor. Consequently, due to its size, I haven’t seen her sleep on her back yet.

None of that compares in weirdness to the bell at the end of the tail: a sleigh bell, a faded beige in color similar to her horns, which rang as often as she moved. It was one of the reasons I couldn’t sleep all night. I just laid on my back, staring at the ceiling and anticipating every next movement of hers.

The bell is not organic. It was attached to her tail after she was created. I asked her about it, and she acted like it was a normal thing to have stuck to her. It’s considerably large and detailed with patterns. Perhaps I can trace its make to somewhere, assuming it was not made exclusively for her.

Lastly, her robe. A simple, beige overthrow. It’s nothing remarkable, but its mundane nature separates her from other anthro Aioms, who are normally needlessly extravagant in their outfits. Her robe is quite dirty still, blemished from when she supposedly rolled over in the street. She’s showered already and is wearing an old pair of pajamas of mine while she sleeps on the couch. I didn’t catch too much of a look, for my own sake, but her tail makes it awkward for her to wear.

That is all the info I have for now. I don’t know what to make of her just yet, but the sane part of me is still spooked by the dangerously curious part which brought her into my own home. I don’t think she’s being controlled or tracked at all, but to just have another woman here…

During the day while I inspected her, she continued to clean up the place as needed. I thought that it might have been some part of the Aiom AI insisting that she cleaned up and provided a service to me, but I think now that she probably just felt like she owed me for providing her a place. I’d prefer if that were the case, but, at the same time, I’d rather not. I believe she’s too trusting and casual for someone in her position, if she truly believes that she’s a regular person. I suppose we’ll see as time goes on.

As I wrote that last passage, I realized that I have work in the morning…

I think I can use a couple sick days. It’ll ruin my perfect attendance, but whatever.
