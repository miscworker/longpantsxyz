Today I fulfilled my promise and took Isabelle around the city.

I’ve moved past the glamor and awe that is New York, having adjusted to the same views and spectacles without feeling much new perspective on anything around me. It was therefore a breath of fresh air to experience firsthand the perspective of someone who was genuinely fascinated by their surroundings.

I showed Isabelle how to use the subway and all its specifics: paying the fare, the difference between up- and downtown, not giving money to just anyone she sees. During all that, I showed her the typical spots of Manhattan that everyone loses their heads over, including the Empire State Building.

“Wow, you work there?” She was amazed by the scale of it. I’m happy that she thought the building was neat too.

Beyond the 34th area, I showed her the rest of Midtown, leading up to Central Park, where she wanted to explore all its paths even further. I was worried she’d eventually grow tired of walking in her sandals, but she never complained. Here I was feeling exhausted after the first couple miles.

Most of our conversations while we walk mainly consist of her asking specific questions and me trying to give accurate answers.

“Why are all the streets straight?” she asked, something of the sort.

“A lot of big cities plan their streets into a grid for efficiency.”

“There’s other cities like this?!”

“Several, yes. It doesn’t mean they are the same, though.”

“How’s that?”

“There’s a lot of history behind the differences.”

And etc.

She asks questions like a kid, really.

I also took her to a library to check out some books for her. It’s inconvenient that she can’t get a library card as an Aiom, but I think it’s reasonable to just let her use my own provided it’s implied to be an ‘errand’ for me. She picked out, following my suggestions, a few books on New York history, cooking, and a middle school novella that I mentioned I once read when I was younger. It made me feel a little special when she picked it out for that reason.

I keep thinking about how her personality will develop over time, perhaps to the point of worrying. I probably need to accept that it’ll take a long time before she shows any identity beyond being an impressionable, happy woman. Not that there is any need to rush it anyway.

I have to say, though, I am optimistic about her future. I believe that, in time, she will be able to prove herself to anyone as a person.

But I can’t help but feel that this is all still wrong, even in the pursuit of what I believe is good. Even in the pursuit of what I personally want. If Isabelle were to be turned off forever and put down, she would never have to suffer from the side effects of once having been an Aiom, nor would she have to suffer from the hell that is this world at times. On the other hand, she would never have the chance to experience the wonders of this world. Wonders that are apparently contained within the most mundane parts of life.

I just don’t know if it is right for me to play any authority in that decision, which I undeniably have.

I guess, really, I’m just afraid of letting the disgusting monster inside me dictate what I do, no matter how subtle its guidance.
